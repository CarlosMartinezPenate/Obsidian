
## Goal
Sterilize clean glassware and gauze stoppers for dinoflagellate culture work.
## Scope
Use this protocol for:
- clean borosilicate / Pyrex glassware
- Erlenmeyer flasks
- culture bottles
- gauze stoppers / gauze closures
- other dry autoclavable items

Do **not** use this protocol for:
- liquid media
- ASW or water bottles
- sealed bottles
- non-autoclavable plastics
- unknown materials
- contaminated biological waste, unless following the lab waste protocol

## References
- [[Tuttnauer 5075 ELVC Manual]]
- [[Cleaning Glassware]]

## Program selection

For clean glassware with gauze stoppers / gauze closures, use:

- **Program 6: Wrapped delicate instruments**
- **121°C**
- **20 min**

This protocol is for **dry glassware and gauze**, not liquids.

Do **not** use:
- **Program 9: Liquid A** unless autoclaving liquids in unsealed bottles
- **Program 10: Liquid B** unless autoclaving liquids in sealed bottles, according to lab practice

## Temperature sensor note

The manual states that for **liquid loads**, the two temperature sensors should be placed into **two separate liquid containers**.

For this protocol, the load is **dry glassware + gauze**, so:

- do **not** place the temperature sensors inside empty flasks
- do **not** move the sensors unless instructed by the lab SOP or trained user
- check that sensors are not trapped, bent, touching the door, or interfering with the load

Simple rule:

**Liquid cycle → sensors go into two separate liquid containers.**

**Dry glassware/gauze cycle → sensors do not go into empty flasks.**

## Safety notes
- Use only autoclavable materials.
- Do not overload the chamber.
- Do not seal bottles or flasks tightly.
- Use heat-resistant gloves when unloading.
- Do not lean over the autoclave door when opening.
- Do not place hands or body over the door.
- If the cycle is aborted before sterilization is complete, treat the load as **not sterile**.
- Allow hot glassware to cool before handling or storage.

## Materials
- Clean dry glassware
- Gauze stoppers / gauze closures
- Aluminum foil, autoclave-safe caps, or wrapping material
- Autoclave tape / sterilization indicator
- Heat-resistant gloves
- Tray or basket
- Marker / labels

## Pre-autoclave preparation

1. Confirm glassware has been cleaned according to [[Cleaning Glassware]].
2. Inspect each item for:
   - visible residue
   - soap film
   - cracks
   - chips
   - remaining liquid
3. Reject and rewash any glassware with visible residue.
4. Do not autoclave cracked or damaged glassware.
5. Make sure glassware is dry or mostly dry before preparing it for sterilization.

## Preparing glassware with gauze stoppers

Preferred setup for culture flasks:

1. Place the gauze stopper / gauze closure on the clean flask before autoclaving.
2. Keep the gauze in the same position it will have during culture use.
3. Make sure the gauze is not packed too tightly.
4. Cover the flask mouth loosely with aluminum foil if needed.
5. Do **not** seal vessels airtight.
6. Apply autoclave tape to the foil, flask, or tray.

This setup sterilizes the flask mouth and gauze together and reduces extra handling after autoclaving.

## Preparing separate gauze stoppers

Use this only if gauze stoppers are not autoclaved directly on the flasks.

1. Prepare gauze stoppers in the form they will be used for culture closure.
2. Keep gauze clean and dry before autoclaving.
3. Wrap gauze stoppers in aluminum foil or place them in an autoclavable container/pouch.
4. Add autoclave tape or another sterilization indicator.
5. Arrange gauze so steam can penetrate.
6. Do not pack gauze too tightly.

## Loading the autoclave

1. Turn on the autoclave using the main switch.
2. Open the door by moving the handle from **locked** to **unlocked**.
3. Load the autoclave basket/tray.
4. Do not overload the chamber.
5. Leave space between items for steam circulation.
6. Make sure glassware is stable and will not tip over.
7. Confirm the temperature sensors are not inside empty flasks.
8. Confirm the temperature sensors are not trapped, bent, or blocking the load.
9. Select **Program 6: Wrapped delicate instruments, 121°C, 20 min**.
10. Verify the selected program before closing the door.

## Starting the cycle

1. Close the autoclave door.
2. Move the handle from **unlocked** to **locked**.
3. Confirm the display shows **System Ready**.
4. Press **START/STOP** to start the cycle.
5. Do not leave questionable or unstable loads running unattended unless allowed by lab practice.

## Cycle completion

At the end of the cycle:

1. Confirm the display shows **Cycle Ended**.
2. Verify that chamber pressure is **0** or that there is no pressure according to the display.
3. Open the door carefully.
4. Do not lean over the door.
5. Keep hands and body away from the steam path.
6. Use heat-resistant gloves or the tray handle.

## Unloading

1. Remove the load carefully.
2. Do not touch hot glassware directly.
3. Place the load in a low-traffic area to cool.
4. Do not place hot glassware directly on cold or wet surfaces.
5. Do not transfer hot items to metal shelves for cooling.
6. Allow the load to cool to room temperature before storage or use.

## Post-autoclave inspection

Inspect the load after sterilization.

Accept the load only if:
- autoclave indicator changed to the required color
- wrapping is intact
- gauze is dry
- glassware is dry
- no condensed drops are visible
- nothing fell on the floor
- no item broke or tipped over
- the cycle completed successfully

Reject or reprocess the load if:
- sterilization indicator did not change correctly
- wrapping is torn
- gauze or packaging is wet
- condensed drops are visible
- the load fell on the floor
- glassware broke
- the cycle failed or was aborted before sterilization was complete

## Storage after autoclaving

1. Store sterile glassware and gauze in a clean, dry area.
2. Keep openings covered until use.
3. Do not touch sterile gauze or flask mouths with bare hands.
4. If sterility is uncertain, re-autoclave before use.
5. Label stored items with:
   - date autoclaved
   - contents
   - initials
   - cycle/program, if required by the lab

## Aborted or failed cycle

If the cycle is stopped or fails before completing the sterilization stage:

- treat the load as **not sterile**
- do not use the items for culture work
- handle as contaminated or non-sterile material
- repeat the autoclave cycle after correcting the problem
