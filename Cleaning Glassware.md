# Glassware Cleaning Protocol

## Purpose
To clean reusable laboratory glassware using DI water and LiquiNox detergent, removing visible residues, detergent traces, and contaminants that may interfere with laboratory work.

## Scope
This procedure applies to routine reusable glassware used in the lab, unless a specific experiment, sterile workflow, chemical contamination, or analytical method requires a separate cleaning procedure.

## Materials
- DI water
- LiquiNox detergent
- Clean wash basin or container
- Appropriate glassware brushes
- Drying rack
- Gloves and lab coat

## Procedure

1. **Pre-rinse**
   - Rinse glassware with DI water to remove loose material.
   - If heavy residue is present, soak before scrubbing.

2. **Prepare detergent solution**
   - Prepare a dilute LiquiNox solution in DI water.
   - Use approximately **1% LiquiNox**, unless the lab has a different approved dilution.
   - Mix gently.

3. **Wash**
   - Dip or soak the glassware in the LiquiNox solution.
   - Scrub all internal and external surfaces with the appropriate brush.
   - Pay attention to corners, bottoms, necks, threads, and areas with visible residue.

4. **First DI rinse**
   - Rinse thoroughly with DI water to remove detergent and loosened material.

5. **Second DI rinse**
   - Rinse again thoroughly with DI water.

6. **Final DI rinse**
   - Perform a final DI water rinse, especially for glassware used for culture media, measurements, or sensitive work.

7. **Drain and dry**
   - Place glassware upside down or at an angle on a clean drying rack.
   - Allow to air-dry completely.
   - Avoid placing clean glassware in contact with dirty bench surfaces.

8. **Inspection**
   - Inspect dry or draining glassware for:
     - visible residue
     - soap film
     - particles
     - stains
     - water beading

9. **Water-break check**
   - Clean glassware should show a continuous sheet of DI water on the surface.
   - If water beads into droplets, the glassware may still contain grease or residue.

10. **Rewash criteria**
   - Rewash any glassware with visible residue, detergent film, particles, stains that matter for the work, or failed water-break behavior.
