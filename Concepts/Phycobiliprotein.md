Phycobiliproteins are deep-colored, water-soluble accessory pigments that include [[phycoerythrins]], [[phycocyanins]], and [[allophycocyanin]], formed by proteins covalently linked to phycobilins.

Phycobiliproteins are composed of proteins and covalently bound via cysteine amino acid chromophores called [phycobilins](https://www-sciencedirect-com.ezproxy.haifa.ac.il/topics/agricultural-and-biological-sciences/phycobilin "Learn more about phycobilins from ScienceDirect's AI-generated Topic Pages"), belonging to open-chain tetrapyrroles (Eriksen, 2008; de Jesus Raposo et al., 2013; Watanabe and Ikeuchi, 2013; Mulders et al., 2014).

Phycobiliproteins can be divided into three large classes depending on their composition and chromophore content: (1) phycocyanins (610–625 nm), (2) [phycoerythrins](https://www-sciencedirect-com.ezproxy.haifa.ac.il/topics/agricultural-and-biological-sciences/phycoerythrin "Learn more about phycoerythrins from ScienceDirect's AI-generated Topic Pages") (490–570 nm), and (3) allophycocyanins (650–660 nm) (Noreña-Caro & Benton, 2018). Phycobiliproteins are bright blue (phycocyanin and allophycoerythrin) and [fuchsia](https://www-sciencedirect-com.ezproxy.haifa.ac.il/topics/agricultural-and-biological-sciences/fuchsia "Learn more about fuchsia from ScienceDirect's AI-generated Topic Pages") (phycorerythrin) and have high commercial value as natural colorants in the [nutraceutical](https://www-sciencedirect-com.ezproxy.haifa.ac.il/topics/agricultural-and-biological-sciences/nutraceutical "Learn more about nutraceutical from ScienceDirect's AI-generated Topic Pages"), cosmetic, medical, and pharmaceutical industries as well as in the textile and food industries.
