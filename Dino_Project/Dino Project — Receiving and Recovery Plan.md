## Purpose

Receive the incoming axenic dinoflagellate strains safely, minimize stress and contamination, and run a simple **IMK vs F/2** comparison over the first **1–2 weeks**. [[Xiang]] et al. maintained source cultures in **liquid IMK at 24–26°C and ~15 µmol photons m^-2 s^-1**. **F/2** was part of the clonal/axenic plating workflow.

## Media roles

### IMK
Primary reference condition for **liquid recovery and maintenance**.

### F/2
**Backup and comparison medium**. Xiang used F/2 in the clonal/axenic workflow, and the Parkinson protocol gives concrete local handling details for preparing and transferring F/2 cultures.

### Glucose / casein hydrolysate
Not default additives for all strains, because Xiang showed strong strain-specific responses:
- glucose: **5 g/L**
- casein hydrolysate: **4 g/L**

### Strain-specific media + requirements 

| **Strain**       | **Priority Media**         | **Notes**                                                             |
| ---------------- | -------------------------- | --------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **SSA01, SSA03** | IMK vs. F/2                | Standard comparison.                                                  |
| **SSA02**        | **IMK + Casein + Glucose** | **Crucial:** Standard IMK/F2 might not sustain this strain long-term. |
| **SSB01**        | IMK (Low Glucose)          | Watch for the "bleaching" effect you noted at 5 g/L glucose.          |
| **SSE01**        | IMK only                   | Avoid Casein here as you noted it depresses growth.                   |

## A. Before arrival

### A1. Prepare F/2 medium and ASW
#### IMK
Use Tingting’s Daigo stock to prepare IMK as soon as the shipment arrives. Xiang used **0.252 g/L Daigo’s IMK powder in ASW** as the base IMK condition.

#### ASW
- Prepare ASW
- Autoclave
- Chill before use

#### F/2
Use the Parkinson Lab workflow for local F/2 preparation:
- ASW at about **32–34 ppt**
- filter through **0.2 µm**
- add **Guillard’s F/2 ingredients** without silica
- dispense **5 mL for tubes** or **30 mL for flasks**
- autoclave liquid media on the liquid cycle
- cool before use

### A2. Prepare labeling and tracking
Pre-label all receiving vessels with:
- strain
- medium
- date
- replicate
- initials

Create a log for:
- strain
- arrival condition
- starting volume
- initial count
- vessel type
- medium
- incubator position
- notes

## B. Arrival

### B1. Inspect shipment
For each tube:
- photograph before opening
- record label / strain
- record arrival volume
- record color
- record clumping / settling
- record obvious contamination or leakage
- record whether the medium looks clear, pigmented, bleached, or turbid

### B2. Quick checks
- **Microscopy check**
	- Check for cell integrity, motility if visible, and obvious debris. Consider DAPI staining + DIC.
- **Hemocytometer count**
	- Estimate starting density
- **Flow cytometry check**
	- If feasible, estimate the starting density and chlorophyll fluorescence
- **Contamination check**
	- Inoculate a small amount (**100 µL**) of the incoming culture into a rich medium such as **Marine Broth** or **LB** to test whether contamination may have occurred during transit
	- PCR test if feasible

## C. Recovery setup

### C1. Media comparison recovery setup
If Tingting’s Daigo stock arrives as expected, for each strain:
- Keep **one reserve aliquot** with minimal handling
- inoculate **IMK**
- inoculate **F/2**
This gives a direct **IMK vs F/2** comparison from the start.
#### C1.1 Aliquot definition
An **aliquot** means **0.5–1.0 mL** of incoming culture per condition.

Recommended default per strain:
- **1 mL into IMK**
- **1 mL into F/2**
- **3 mL kept as reserve**

---
#### C1.2. Vessel

##### Option 1 — Tube-based startup
Use sterile tubes with a final volume of **5 mL per condition**, consistent with the [[Symbiodiniaceae Culturing Protocols – Parkinson Lab]].
- **Tube 1:** 1 mL incoming culture + 4 mL IMK
- **Tube 2:** 1 mL incoming culture + 4 mL F/2
- **Tube 3:** 3 mL reserve in original tube or fresh sterile tube
##### Option 2 — Flask-based startup
Transfer to small sterile flasks with a final volume of **~30 mL**, as in the [[Symbiodiniaceae Culturing Protocols – Parkinson Lab]].

---

### C2. Recovery incubation setup

**Suggestion:** For the first **24–48 hours**, consider keeping them in **very low light** to allow recovery from transport stress before ramping up to Xiang et al. maintenance levels.
- **24–26°C**
- **12:12 light: dark** or stable low-light regime
- around **15 µmol photons m^-2 s^-1**, if possible

## D. Long-term parent culture maintenance
####  Routine maintenance

- Initially:
    - Maintain on **F/2 + antibiotics plates**
    - Passage ~**monthly**
- Later (once clean):
    - Switch to an **antibiotic-free medium**
    - Continue **monthly passaging**
    - Routinely check for clonal isolation
