## Glycerol stock solution

Jenia showed me the glycerol stock solution used for bacterial storage.

**Composition:**
- 50% glycerol
- 50% ddH₂O

**Preparation:**
- Mix glycerol and ddH₂O 1:1
- Autoclave before use

---

## Preparation of bacterial glycerol stocks

Jenia prepared glycerol stocks from a liquid bacterial culture.

### Workflow observed

1. Work was performed in the biological hood.
2. A bacterial culture was used as the cell source.
3. Jenia mixed:
   - 2 mL sterile 50% glycerol stock solution
   - 2 mL bacterial culture

This gives a final glycerol concentration of approximately **25% glycerol**.

4. The mixture was aliquoted into sterile Eppendorf tubes.
5. Each aliquot contained **150 µL**.
6. Aliquoting was done using a **5 mL serological pipette**.

---

## Flash freezing procedure

Jenia also demonstrated the flash freezing step.

### Liquid nitrogen handling

1. Liquid nitrogen was collected from the storage container in the neighboring lab.
2. The container was a tilting metal liquid nitrogen vessel.
3. Safety glasses were worn.
4. Liquid nitrogen was poured into a smaller flask/dewar-like container.

### Freezing aliquots

1. The aliquoted Eppendorf tubes were brought close to the −80°C freezer.
2. The tubes were dropped into liquid nitrogen.
3. After freezing, the tubes were retrieved using a stainless-steel spoon/strainer tool.
4. Frozen aliquots were transferred into a cryobox.
5. The cryobox was stored in the −80°C freezer.

---

## Important notes

- Final glycerol concentration was ~25%, suitable for bacterial long-term storage.
- Work should remain sterile until tubes are closed.
- Liquid nitrogen handling requires eye protection and care.
- Tubes should be clearly labeled before freezing.
- Frozen stocks should remain at −80°C and should not be repeatedly thawed.

---

## Key concept

The glycerol protects cells during freezing by reducing ice-crystal damage. Flash freezing rapidly brings the culture to cryogenic temperature, and −80°C storage maintains the cells for future recovery.
