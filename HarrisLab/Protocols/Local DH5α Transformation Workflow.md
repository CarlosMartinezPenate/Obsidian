# Local DH5α Transformation Workflow
### Adapted from the [[https://www.addgene.org/protocols/bacterial-transformation| Addgene]] bacterial transformation protocol and Jenia’s lab workflow.

1. Prepare antibiotic LB agar plates in advance.
2. Keep plates sealed in their bags while outside the biological hood.
3. Take plates and LB medium from the fridge.
4. Aliquot 500 µL LB into sterile Eppendorf tubes.
5. Place the plates and LB tubes at 37 °C to pre-warm.
6. Prepare an ice bucket.
7. Take plasmids from the fridge and place them on ice.
8. Briefly spin plasmid tubes to bring them to the bottom.
9. Dilute plasmids with RNase/DNase-free water to the desired working concentration (200 ng/uL).
10. Mix plasmid dilutions by gently pipetting up and down.
11. Take DH5α competent cells from -80 °C.
12. Place competent cells immediately on ice.
13. Cool empty sterile Eppendorf tubes on ice.
14. Thaw competent cells on ice for ~30 min.
15. Briefly pulse-spin thawed competent cells if needed.
16. Gently mix competent cells by pipetting up and down.
17. Transfer 50 µL competent cells into each pre-chilled Eppendorf tube.
18. Add 1 µL plasmid DNA to the corresponding tube.
19. Gently mix.
20. Incubate tubes on ice for 30 min.
21. Heat shock at 42 °C for 45 sec in dry bath.
22. Return tubes immediately to ice for 5 min.
23. Transfer cells into corresponding tubes containing 500 µL pre-warmed LB.
24. Mix gently by pipetting up and down.
25. Incubate at 37 °C for 1 h.
26. Label plates with strain, plasmid/protein, antibiotic, date, and initials.
27. In the biological hood, plate 150 µL transformation mixture onto the matching antibiotic plate.
28. Spread using sterile plastic rake/spreader.
29. Incubate plates at 37 °C overnight.
30. Check colonies the next day.
31. Store the parafilm wrapped plates in the fridge
