# Symbiodiniaceae Culturing Protocols – Parkinson Lab

**Last Saved Update:** 2026-06-11

## Cleaning Glassware

1. Only use DI water for cleaning.
2. Prepare soapy solution with LiquiNox soap (small amount).
3. Dip glassware and scrub with brushes.
4. Rinse off soap.
5. Rinse off soap again!
6. Add to drying rack.
7. Allow to dry then check for residue--if present, wash that glassware again.

## Preparing Glass Pipettes

1. Stuff glass pipette tips with a thin layer of cotton using forceps. There should be enough cotton to seal the apical ring, but it doesn't need to completely fill the top portion.
2. Place pipettes in autoclavable metal boxes.
3. Place boxes into appropriate bin (heat-resistant plastic).
4. Use autoclave on the 3rd floor. Record info on the data sheet. Don’t touch the sides of the autoclave (use gloves to be safe). Use digital display to open/close door. Stand back whenever opening/closing in case steam escapes quickly.
5. If only autoclaving pipettes, set autoclave to grav/30/121/15 (30 minutes of processing with a 15 minute dry down--allow an additional 10 minutes for cooling, so ~1 hour total). If only autoclaving pipettes, choose the 'gravity' setting (If also autoclaving liquid media, use liq/30/121. This has no dry down period.).
6. Using gloves, open autoclave door, remove bin, then close door.
7. Allow pipettes to cool to room temperature before use.

## Preparing Media

1. Make artificial seawater (~28 mL volume of salt per 1 L DI water to get ~32 ppt (32-34 ppt acceptable; check with refractometer).
2. Filter the seawater (use pump and 0.2 micron filter).
3. Add F/2 ingredients (in fridge; don’t use silica).
4. Add media to the clean glassware (5 mL for tubes, 30 mL for flasks).
5. Use autoclave on the 3rd floor. Record info on the data sheet. Don’t touch the sides of the autoclave (use gloves to be safe). Use digital display to open/close door. Stand back whenever opening/closing in case steam escapes quickly.
6. When autoclaving anything with liquid media in it, set autoclave to liq/30/121 (30 minutes of processing with no dry down--allow an additional 25 minutes for cooling, so ~1 hour total). You can also autoclave pipette tips at the same time on the same setting.
7. Using gloves, open autoclave door, remove bin, then close door.
8. Allow media to cool to room temperature before use. This process can be sped up by placing glassware in the refrigerator.

## Performing Transfers

1. Pre-label transfer vials (borosilicate glass tubes).
2. Place new vials next to original vials in rack (use differential placement while processing make sure you're transferring to the correct vial).
3. Perform transfers in a biosafety box (not the hood!) to avoid needing a flame. Alternatively, set up and ethanol flame.
4. Take out a single pipette with bulb in right hand and a single original vial in left (or vice-versa if you are left-handed).
5. Unscrew cap, keeping it in the right hand.
6. With the left, flame the lip of the vial (if using a flame instead of a biosafety box).
7. Insert pipette, scrape and mix vial contents, and pull up a small amount of algae (liquid level should reach just beyond the curve of the glass).
8. Remove pipette (don't discharge contents).
9. Flame the lip again (if needed) and screw cap back on.
10. Return vial to rack (offset, so you know it's done).
11. Pick up appropriate transfer vial.
12. Unscrew cap, keeping it in the right hand.
13. With the left, flame the lip of the vial (if needed).
14. Insert pipette and expel contents into transfer vial.
15. Remove pipette, flame the lip (if needed), screw cap back on.
16. Place transfer vial in rack in an offset position.
17. Repeat for all cultures.

## Incubator Conditions

- Light: 40-70 umol (set to 35% of maximum output from 10000K fluorescent white lamps)
- Diel Cycle: 12:12 light:dark (starting at 9 am)
- Temperature: 26 °C
