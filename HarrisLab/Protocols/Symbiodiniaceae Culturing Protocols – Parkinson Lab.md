## Cleaning Glassware

1. Use only **DI water** for cleaning.
2. Prepare a soapy solution with a small amount of **LiquiNox**.
3. Dip glassware and scrub with brushes.
4. Rinse off the soap.
5. Rinse off the soap **again**.
6. Place glassware on the drying rack.
7. Allow to dry, then inspect for residue. If residue is present, wash that glassware again.

## Preparing Glass Pipettes

1. Stuff the tips of the glass pipettes with a thin layer of cotton using forceps.
	- Use enough cotton to seal the apical ring.
	- It does not need to completely fill the top portion.
2. Place the pipettes in autoclavable metal boxes.
3. Place the boxes into the appropriate heat-resistant plastic bin.
4. Use the autoclave on the 3rd floor.
	- Record the run information on the data sheet.
	- Do not touch the sides of the autoclave.
	- Use gloves to be safe.
	- Use the digital display to open and close the door.
	- Stand back whenever opening or closing the autoclave in case steam escapes quickly.
5. If autoclaving **only pipettes**, set the autoclave to:
	- `grav/30/121/15`
	- 30 minutes processing
	- 15 minutes dry down
	- allow an additional ~10 minutes for cooling
	- total time: about **1 hour**
	- choose the **gravity** setting
6. If also autoclaving **liquid media**, use:
	- `liq/30/121`
	- no dry down period
7. Using gloves, open the autoclave door, remove the bin, then close the door.
8. Allow the pipettes to cool to room temperature before use.

## Preparing Media

1. Make artificial seawater:
	- use about **28 mL volume of salt per 1 L DI water**
	- target salinity: about **32 ppt**
	- **32–34 ppt** is acceptable
	- verify with a refractometer
2. Filter the seawater using:
	- a pump
	- a **0.2 µm filter**
3. Add **F/2 ingredients** from the fridge.
	- **Do not use silica.**
4. Add media to clean glassware:
	- **5 mL** for tubes
	- **30 mL** for flasks
5. Use the autoclave on the 3rd floor.
	- Record the run information on the data sheet.
	- Do not touch the sides of the autoclave.
	- Use gloves to be safe.
	- Use the digital display to open and close the door.
	- Stand back whenever opening or closing the autoclave in case steam escapes quickly.
6. When autoclaving anything with liquid media, set the autoclave to:
	- `liq/30/121`
	- 30 minutes processing
	- no dry down
	- allow an additional ~25 minutes for cooling
	- total time: about **1 hour**
	- pipette tips can also be autoclaved at the same time on this setting
7. Using gloves, open the autoclave door, remove the bin, then close the door.
8. Allow the media to cool to room temperature before use.
	- This can be sped up by placing the glassware in the refrigerator.

## Performing Transfers

1. Pre-label transfer vials (**borosilicate glass tubes**).
2. Place new vials next to the original vials in the rack.
	- Use differential placement while processing so you can track which vial is being transferred.
3. Perform transfers in a **biosafety box** rather than the hood, to avoid needing a flame.
	- Alternatively, set up and use an **ethanol flame**.
4. Take one pipette with bulb in the right hand and one original vial in the left.
	- Reverse hands if left-handed.
5. Unscrew the cap, keeping it in the right hand.
6. With the left hand, flame the lip of the vial if using a flame.
7. Insert the pipette, scrape and mix the vial contents, and pull up a small amount of algae.
	- The liquid level should reach just beyond the curve of the glass.
8. Remove the pipette without discharging the contents.
9. Flame the lip again if needed, then screw the cap back on.
10. Return the original vial to the rack in an offset position so you know it is done.
11. Pick up the appropriate transfer vial.
12. Unscrew the cap, keeping it in the right hand.
13. With the left, flame the lip of the vial if needed.
14. Insert the pipette and expel the contents into the transfer vial.
15. Remove the pipette, flame the lip if needed, then screw the cap back on.
16. Place the transfer vial in the rack in an offset position.
17. Repeat for all cultures.

## Incubator Conditions

- **Light:** 40–70 µmol
	- set to **35% of maximum output**
	- using **10000K fluorescent white lamps**
- **Diel cycle:** 12:12 light:dark
	- starting at **9:00 AM**
- **Temperature:** **26 °C**
