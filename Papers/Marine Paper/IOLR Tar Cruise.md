To evaluate to which degree our candidate indicators proposed in our controlled contrasts and field datasets generalize beyond the "cruise 2" sampling campaigns, we examined an independent dataset collected by the Israel Oceanographic and Limnological Research (IOLR; Maxim Rubim) along the Haifa transects during a cruise targeting tar-associated conditions. 

This dataset provides a practical external test of our proposed microbial indicators: whether they are present in the same regional oceanographic setting, in which tar exposure events have been documented. Interestingly, the IOLR has collected samples on the transect regularly and consistently at Station Hertzliya, which represent a historical context. At the same time, it differs in key design features from our primary data: IOLR samples were not size-fractionated and were also not paired with contemporaneous chemical measurements. 

Using a depth-stratified exceedance framework (q99 relative-abundance threshold defined from non-tar cruises within each ds3 bin), only three candidates showed excursions during the tar cruise. ASV67 (Glaciecola; Alteromonadaceae) exceeded q99 exclusively in the near-surface bin (2 exceedances), whereas ASV236/ASV506 (Aurantivirga; Flavobacteriaceae) exceeded q99 in subsurface bins (DCM and below-DCM; 2 exceedances total). ASV448 (NS3a marine group; Flavobacteriaceae) showed broader but weaker excursions across surface, near-surface, and DCM (3 exceedances). The remaining candidates showed no q99 exceedances in any depth bin. These patterns suggest at least two ‘modes’ of anomaly: a near-surface tar-associated excursion (ASV67) and a subsurface excursion (ASV236/ASV506), while ASV448 appears to behave as a more general upper-water-column responder.
